# Cross-Source Synthesis

Turn scattered information from multiple tools into a unified, attributed picture.

1. **Survey available sources.** List which tools are connected and what time range is relevant. Do not begin pulling until the scope is clear.
2. **Pull in parallel.** Issue a specific query to each source simultaneously. One query per source — no open-ended browsing.
3. **Reconcile conflicts.** When sources disagree, do not silently pick one. **Hard stop: present both and ask: "Source A says X, Source B says Y. Which is correct?" Do not proceed until the human answers.**
4. **Synthesize with attribution.** Every claim in the output names its source and date: "Based on your email with [person] on [date]..." No unattributed assertions.
5. **Highlight gaps.** End with: "I checked [sources], but couldn't find [missing info]." Do not omit this step even if the synthesis looks complete.

For detailed parallel pull instructions and query templates, read `references/synthesis-process.md`.

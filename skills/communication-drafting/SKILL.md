# Communication Drafting

1. **Identify the audience.** Before writing anything, answer: Who is the recipient? What is their relationship to the sender? What do they need from this message? What medium is this for?
2. **Select tone and format** using the tone mapping table in `references/drafting-guide.md`. Professional email, casual email, formal document, and quick message each have different norms.
3. **Hard stop before drafting:** Do not write a single sentence until audience, tone, and format are explicitly named. If any is unclear, ask the human and wait for an answer.
4. **Multi-audience rule:** If the same content must reach different audiences, draft each version separately. Do not write one message for everyone.
5. **Draft.** Front-load the key point. Match the medium. Respect the reader's time — shorter is almost always better.
6. **Check the ask.** Every message with a requested action must have a specific, dated ask. "Can you confirm the budget by Friday?" beats "Let me know your thoughts."
7. **Review.** Does the tone match the relationship? Is the ask clear? Is anything sensitive that needs softening or removing?

Tone table and multi-audience process: `references/drafting-guide.md`
